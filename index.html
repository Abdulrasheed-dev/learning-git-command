Here is a list of just some of the litttle things that i look for in others's code and that i try to do my self that make for good craftmanship in HTML.

  • DOCTYPE Properly Decleared
    • It looks like alot of gibbrish, but DOCTYPES are important. They not only allow your code to validate, but they tell browsers things about how to render your pages. Simple (htmt) tags don't cut it.

  • Head Section.
    • Tittle is set. Characters set decleared. stylesheet linked (including a print stylesheet). Script liked and NOT include in full, external files have their own related folders (eg. "css" and "script").

  • Body IDed
    • Putting an ID on your bodies allowed you to creat CSS properties that are unique to the page. for instance, you may want your (h2) tags to look different on the homepage. In your CSS you can write:#home h2{} to accomplish this and not affect (h2) tags elseware.
